Lasagna Recipe

Lasagna

A Picture of Lasagna

In this recipe, you will make the best lasagna that people have eaten in their life! It feeds about 8 people and takes around 105 minutes to make.

Ingredients for Meat Sauce

  • 2 teaspoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 pound ground beef chuck
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced (3/4 cup)
  • 1/2 large bell pepper (any color), diced (3/4 cup)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28-ounce) can of good-quality tomato sauce
  • 3 ounces tomato paste (half a 6-ounce can)
  • 1 (14 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh oregano, or 2 teaspoons of dried oregano
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley (preferably flat leaf), packed
  • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 pinch garlic powder and/or garlic salt
  • 1 tablespoon red or white wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon to 1/4 cup sugar (to taste, optional)
  • Salt

Ingredients for Lasagna Noodles

  • 1/2 pound dry lasagna noodles (requires 9 lasagna noodles - unbroken)
  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 1/2 pounds (24 ounces) mozzarella cheese, grated or sliced
  • 1/4 pound (4 ounces) freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Recipe Steps:

  1. Put pasta water on to boil: Put a large pot of salted water (1 Tbsp salt for every 2 quarts of water) on the stovetop on high heat. This will take a while; prepare the sauce in next steps while water is heating.
  2. Brown the ground beef: In a large skillet heat 2 teaspoons of olive oil on medium high heat. Add ground beef and cook until it is lightly browned on all sides. Remove the beef with a slotted spoon to a bowl. Drain off all but a tablespoon of fat.
  3. Cook the bell pepper, onions, garlic, add back the beef: Add the diced bell pepper and onions to skillet. Cook for 4 to 5 minutes until the onions are translucent and the peppers are softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for another half minute. Return the ground beef to the pan. Stir to combine, reduce the heat to low, and cook for 5 more minutes.
  4. Transfer to medium sized pot, add tomatoes and remaining sauce ingredients to build sauce: Transfer beef mixture to medium-sized pot. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, and tomato paste to pot. Add parsley, oregano, Italian seasonings, and adjust amounts to taste. Sprinkle with garlic powder and/or salt to taste. Sprinkle with red or white wine vinegar. Stir in sugar, a tablespoon at a time, tasting after each addition, to taste. Add salt to taste, but make sure you dont add too much, as you are adding parmesan later, which is salty. Bring sauce to simmer and then lower heat to maintain a low simmer. Cook for 15 to 45 mins, stirring often. Scrape bottom of pot every once in a while to make sure nothing sticks to the bottom. Remove from heat after you are done.
  5. Boil and drain lasagna noodles: Add dry lasagna noodles to the salted water (which should be boiling at this point) and cook them to al dente. Stir often to prevent noodles from sticking. Make sure that water remains at full rolling boil during entire cooking time to prevent noodles from sticking. When ready, drain in colander and rinse with cool water, gently separating any noodles that are sticking together. Spread a little olive oil on a large rimmed baking sheet, and lay out the cooked noodles on this sheet, turning them over so that they get coated with a little olive oil. This will prevent them from sticking together.
  6. Heat oven to 375 Fahrenheit
  7. Assemble Lasagna: In a 9x13 inch casserole or lasagna dish, ladle a cup of sauce and spread it over the bottom of the dish. Arrange one layer of lasagna noodles lengthwise (about 3 long noodles, edge may overlap, depending on your pan) over the sauce. Ladle a third of the remaining sauce over the noodles. Sprinkle a layer of a third of the grated mozzarella on top of lasagna sauce. Add half of the ricotta cheese, by placing cheese dollops every couple inches. Sprinkle half the grated parmesan cheese evenly over the top of ricotta cheese. Add second layer of noodles, top it with half of the remaining sauce. Add half of the remaining mozzarella, the remaining ricotta cheese, and another the remaining Parmesan. Finish with another layer of noodles. Spread the remaining sauce over the top layer of noodles and sprinkle with the remaining mozzarella cheese.
  8. Bake: Cover the lasagna pan with aluminum foil, tented slightly so it doesn't touch the noodles or sauce. Bake at 375 Fahrenheit for 45 minutes. Uncover in the last 10 minutes if you'd like more of a crusty top or edges.
  9. Cool and serve: Allow the lasagna to cool at least 15 minutes before serving. Leftovers will keep for about 5 days. May be reheated in conventional oven or microwave. Leave aluminum tent on for storage. (Try to keep the aluminum foil from touching the sauce)
